Mama's Too

2750 Broadway
(106th Street)
Upper West Side
212-510-7256

Atmosphere :A white-tiled neighborhood slice joint with a handful of counter seats; everything is shiny and new for the time being. You order and pick up at the counter; the system is not a model of efficiency, but it gets the job done. 		Recommended Dishes: House slice; pepperoni square; cacio e pepe square; pear-Gorgonzola square; mozzarella crescents. Regular slice, $3.50; squares, $4.50; regular pies, $21; square pies, $25 to $29.
Drinks and Wine: A handful of bottled beers and Italian sodas. Noise Level: Tranquil, punctuated by pie talk from the kitchen.

Henry at Life Hotel

19 West 31st Street
(Fifth Avenue)
Midtown
212-615-9910
Atmosphere : The bistro-like dining room manages some comfort despite having been shoehorned into a small, busy lobby. Service is warm, welcoming, and on board with the restaurant’s program.
Recommended Dishes: Tuna tartare; roti; piri-piri clams; mushroom yassa; concón; pan roasted scallops; beef short rib; sweet potato pie. Appetizers, $12 to $16; main courses, $28 to $42; higher for dishes that serve more than one.
Drinks and Wine: The sophisticated cocktail menu picks up the kitchen’s global theme, and seems to have been given more thought than the lackluster wine list. Noise Level: Moderately loud

Hunan Slurp

112 First Avenue
(East Seventh Street)
East Village
646-585-9585
Atmosphere : As the long, informal, stylish dining room tunnels back from a picture window, communal tables give way to individual ones. The service is fast and professional.
Recommended Dishes: Marinated cherry tomatoes; chicken feet; Hunan charcuterie; stir-fried cabbage; chicken with young ginger; all noodles. Appetizers, $8 to $18; noodles and main courses (some of which will feed two or more), $15 to $38.
Drinks and Wine: No alcohol is served, but the teas are better than average. Noise Level:Taxing at times.

Atomix

104 East 30th Street
(Park Avenue South),
Murray Hill

Atmosphere : Upstairs is an airy, civilized bar for snacks and cocktails; downstairs is a snug, Zen-inspired 14-seat tasting counter. Service is warm, gracious and eager to share the stories behind the food and the setting. 		Recommended Dishes: Bar: scallop jerky; beef tartare; chicken wings. Dining room: The 10-course tasting menu is the only option. Bar: $7 to $15. Dining room: $175.
Drinks and Wine :The wine list is deep and rewarding, if not particularly value-focused; selections can be paired course-for-course with the menu for $155. The Korean teas and infusions, such as wild pear and white-lotus leaf, are exceptional. Noise Level:Ambient music plays at ambient volumes.

Financial District
212-230-5788


Atmosphere : A restrained, almost somber dining room where the thrills are provided by floor-to-ceiling windows facing north and east toward Brooklyn.
